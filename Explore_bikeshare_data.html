Question 1¶

On which week days were bikes most often rented in Chicago? And is there a difference in usage behaviour by customer type?

Approach: (1) Convert Start.Time to week days and (2) generate stacked bar chart

In [17]:
ggplot(aes(Start.Time_WD), data=chi)+
  geom_bar(color='blue', fill='blue')+
  xlab('week days')+ylab('number of bike rentals')+
  facet_wrap(~User.Type)

Bikes are most often rented on Mondays in Chicago. Interestingly there is a difference between regular customers (subscribers) and non-regular customers (customers). Regular customers (subscribers) rent bikes more often during the working days whereas non-regular customers tend to rent bikes more often during the weekend in Chicago.

  geom_point(alpha=0.05, color='green')+
  coord_cartesian(xlim=c(18,80), ylim=c(1,60))+
  xlab('age')+ylab('trip duration in min')+
  geom_line(stat='summary', fun.y=median)+
  geom_line(stat='summary', fun.y=quantile, fun.args=list(probs=.9), linetype=3, color='red')

No, users between 20 and 60 years have a very similar average trip duration. The median is about 10 minutes per trip. 90% of all trip durations for persons in this age range are between 1 and 25 minutes.
